** Introduction

- Claims that in model-free responding "Only valence and not quality
  of stimuli are represented (Dayan & Berridge, 2014)."

- Revaluation seen as indicator of model-based responding.

- Check [[cite:&dickinson1979appetitive]] for Konorski's account

** Hypotheses

- Hp 1: :: "after initial training the organism has associated the CS
  with a sensory representation of the UCS (e.g., the Dead Sea Salt
  solution) and when it is faced with a decision in a new
  physiological state, it can reactivate that representation and use
  the representation to reevaluate it."

- Hp 2 :: "For any given stimulus, there are multiple sensory
  representations associated with the stimulus."

- Hp 3 :: Evaluation of the stimulus (e.g., the salt solution) takes
  place in two independent motivational systems(Dickinson & Balleine,
  2002), an Approach or Appetitive system, and an Avoidance or
  Aversive system
  
** The model

The model is similar to mine but it assumes that the gating is at the
level of reward calculations rather than actions! This is why they
still need the model-based part, see equation 5.

The also need to complicate the model when assuming an appetitive and
an aversive system, to explain the fact that even under strong salt
need a supersalty solution is often not preferred to a moderately
